Nachtwacht 

Following their previous works such as Brave New World 2.0 (2019), The Underground (2022), and Yara's Wedding (2023), Rik van den Bos, Guy Weizman, and Roni Haver delve once again into a realm where the significant themes of our era are fearlessly and unconventionally explored through a fusion of dance, text and newly composed music.

Angels in America 

In 2008, Ivo van Hove directed a radical Angels in America which Tony Kushner himself, after his passage through Amsterdam, dubbed his favourite version up to then. It is this version that is now being staged again, with some actors from the original cast and some new actors from the ITA Ensemble.

Omâ, or the priviliges of the potato 

German-Iranian Roxana Küwen Arsalan juggles with her feet in a unique way. She graduated from Fontys Circus in Tilburg in 2013. In ‘OMÂ’ (a combination of the German ‘Oma’ and the Iranian ‘Mâdarjun) she brings her two grandmothers, who have never met in real life, together for a cup of tea. Because it is a solo performance, Roxana plays both her grandmothers in this personal story full of colorful characters, poetry and humor.

Hamlet 

Young Hamlet mourns his deceased father. There seems to be no room for this in his immediate environment. His mother quickly remarries, with her husband's brother. Denying Hamlet's grief affects him in his deepest being. As if reality is sinking under his feet. He becomes entangled in his thoughts and questions everything and everyone around him. He promises one thing - whatever the consequences - he will not forget his father.

Belle and the Beast 

The classic fairy tale Beauty and the Beast was written in the 18th century and is one of the most widely read fairy tales and the basis for many books, films and theatre performances. When the father of a close-knit family has to travel to secure the family's happiness, he ends up in a castle. In the gardens he picks a rose for his daughter Belle. Then his host appears: a Beast, who wants Belle to come to his castle. Will Belle realize in time that the Beast is actually a prince, who can only be saved by true love?

Hè Fijn 

Here we are. On a floating sphere in an infinitely large universe. We have no idea what the purpose is. For years we have been able to do whatever we wanted: eat, drink, party... it was endless. But now that the world seems to be slowly moving towards the abyss, we have to grow up. We have to start controlling ourselves. And that is exactly what Martijn Crins has been struggling with his entire life. After Mesthoop, comedian Martijn Crins takes on his urges in Hè Fijn. Honest, brutal and idiosyncratic, he looks firmly into the blind spot of our existence.
